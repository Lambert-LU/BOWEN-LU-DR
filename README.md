# European Aviation Market Analysis (Unsupervised Learning & Dimensionality Reduction)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes the European aviation market (2013–2024) using unsupervised learning and dimensionality reduction techniques based on Eurostat data.

The objective is to uncover latent structures in high-dimensional country–year data and translate statistical findings into interpretable market insights.

---

## Key Questions
- Can the structure of the aviation market be identified without labels?
- Are market dynamics driven by a unified system or by individual countries?
- Can dimensionality reduction reveal structural breaks and market cycles?

---

## Methodology
- Data preprocessing (cleaning, transformation, standardization)
- Principal Component Analysis (PCA)
- Temporal structure analysis

---

## Key Findings
- The European aviation market behaves as a highly integrated system  
- A dominant systemic factor explains most of the variation  
- COVID-19 represents a clear structural break  
- Post-2022 recovery demonstrates strong system-wide resilience  
- Non-EU countries show weaker integration and higher heterogeneity  

---

## Business Implications
- The EU aviation market should be analyzed as a system, not as independent countries  
- Strategic decisions should focus on market-wide cycles rather than local fluctuations  
- Systemic shocks propagate rapidly in highly integrated markets  

---

## Strategic Insight
In a highly integrated market, competitive advantage depends on understanding system-wide dynamics rather than optimizing country-level positioning.
